What is a Vignette?

Researchers systematically manipulate factors to understand their composite and individual effects on a decision [22, 26]. This reliable method is used by social and decision scientists and applied across psychology, sociology, and marketing, to name a few [2, 26]. Factorial vignettes are proven more effective to understanding decision making than direct questioning or single statement ratings that obscure the underlying contributions of different factors to the overall decision [1, 22, 26]. Factorial vignettes are presented in surveys using a basic template that contains multiple dimensions of the construct of interest.

The perception of a paragraph changes depending on the adjectives within it and its subjects. For example, if the sentence was "A boy lost his ball and he cried." you may feel sad and sympathize with him. However, if the author changed cried to laughed the scenario changes in its entirety. Within security experts describing a network connection as being a home network as opposed to a public network would raise different opinions on the safety of checking bank account balance. As such, Vignettes in this application are short scenarios with different variables that can be described by varying the level of perceived security. By changing variables in this way we are able to ask security experts to evaluate the overall security of a system or a set of interactions. Having security experts craft different vignettes allows for more coverage of scenarios.
