Crafting a Vignette

The Descriptive Statement

A descriptive statement expands the interactive statement. They are statements which describe the level of a technology that affect the overall security level when performing the action. The descriptive statements have $variables which are varied by level to change the perception of the vignette. Some examples of $variables include $NetworkType, $Connection, and $Password.

In order to prompt descriptive sentences, questions are asked such as:

  • How is my data shared?
  • How is the data stored?
  • Who has access to the data?
  • How do I know that I am contacting the correct person?

    • These sentences prompt the expert to think of $variables such as $Connection, $Encryption, $Access, and $Authentication. Then with these $variables in mind, a sentence is crafted including each one. Then the $variable, can be varied in order to illustrate different levels of security. For $Authentication, levels may include basic, dual-factor, and form-based authentication using encrypted credentials stored in a database. In the case of $Connection levels may include TLS 1.2 and SSL 3.0. The expectation is not that they all are extremely secure, but that they vary so that it can be verified if the user who reads the story can identify weak in comparison to strong security factors.

    The interaction statement we will describe is as follows: "As a patient, I'd like to use email to contact my doctor so that I can share the results of my Peak Flow Meter."

    Example descriptive statements:

    • I decide to contact my doctor while I am $Network.
      • I decide to contact my doctor while I am on my home network.
      • I decide to contact my doctor while I am at work.
      • I decide to contact my doctor while I am using public wifi.
    • When I log into my email account I have to enter my $password.
      • When I log into my email account I have to enter my 8 character, alphanumeric password.
      • When I log into my email account I have to enter my 8 character, alphanumeric password that contains no dictionary words.
      • When I log into my email account I have to enter my 12 character, alphanumeric password that contains no dictionary words.
    • The device measures my ability to breathe air out of my lungs. I take measurements twice a day and the doctors office stores the data in $Database.
      • I take measurements twice a day and the doctors office stores the data in mySQL.
      • I take measurements twice a day and the doctors office stores the data in Oracle.
      • I take measurements twice a day and the doctors office stores the data in MongoDB.

    These sentences can then be combined to create several different vignettes varying from the lowest security to the highest security. With each level they should be ranked from 1 to 4 with 1 being least secure to 4 being most secure. For example public wifi would rate at a 1 bur work wifi would likely be ranked a 3.

    Higher Security Vignette

    "As a patient, I'd like to use email to contact my doctor so that I can share the results of my Peak Flow Meter. I decide to contact my doctor while I am on my home network. When I log into my email account I have to enter my 12 character, alphanumeric password that contains no dictionary words.I take measurements twice a day and the doctors office stores the data in Oracle."

    Lower Security Level Vignette

    "As a patient, I'd like to use email to contact my doctor so that I can share the results of my Peak Flow Meter. I decide to contact my doctor while I am using public wifi. When I log into my email account I have to enter my 8 character, alphanumeric password. I take measurements twice a day and the doctors office stores the data in MongoDB.
